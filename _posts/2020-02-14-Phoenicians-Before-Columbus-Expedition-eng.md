It was a long voyage. Ten years ago, the same people who had sailed around Africa aboard a Phoenician ship this time crossed the Atlantic. On September 23, 2019, the ship set out from ancient Carthage, and on February 10, 2020, after a 6,000-mile journey, it arrived in Miami, USA. It took five months. With this, it was proven that the Atlantic could be crossed even with a ship from roughly 3,000 years ago.
